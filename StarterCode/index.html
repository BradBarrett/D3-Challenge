Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks, Income and Obesity

The Chart above tracks state average state income and compares these findings to the average observed obesity rate in the state. The theory being that as incomes increase; households have access to higher quality, less processed food sources.

When the dataset is charted with the X-Axis representing State Income Levels and the Y-Axis representing Obesity Rates we see that the bubbles representing the State start to cluster forming a loose correlation showing that as income increases to the right of the X-Axis the Obesity rate improves upward to smaller rates of obesity.

Looking at a couple specific examples we see clusters of the lowest income states such as MS, AR, & WV are clustered around the highest average recorded levels of obesity.
